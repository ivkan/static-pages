Phase A

Parameter Calibration

The engine studies 16,000+ historical trips and continuously adjusts its pricing parameters — base fares, per-mile rates, city multipliers — until predictions stop improving.

Setting the price foundation

The engine looks at thousands of real transfer trips and calculates the best starting price and per-mile rate for each of the 5 pricing tiers. Each tier is a rate scale assigned to a group of cities with similar cost profiles. Think of it as finding the tuning forks that everything else is calibrated against.

Minimum 20 trips required per pricing tier
Linear regression needs enough data points to produce a statistically reliable trend line. With fewer than 20 trips the fitted base fare and per-mile rate would be dominated by outliers. Tiers with insufficient data keep their existing values unchanged.

Phase B

Tier Reassignment

Some cities end up in the wrong pricing tier. This phase tests all 5 options for each city and switches it to the better one — only if the improvement is significant and doesn't hurt overall accuracy.

Trying on all 5 price scales

For every city with at least 5 historical trips, the system simulates what prices would look like if that city were assigned to each of the 5 tiers. It is like trying 5 different pricing lenses and seeing which one fits — done entirely by math, with no manual input.

Minimum 5 trips per city required to qualify
With fewer than 5 trips, accuracy comparison between tiers is statistically meaningless — a single unusual trip could make the wrong tier look best. Five is the minimum for a meaningful majority pattern.
